Math classes I took to earn my Applied Math Degree

Math Classes I Took
Class Number Class Name Brief description
1 Calculus I A introductory course that most STEM Majors take that introduces foundational calculus content like Limits, Derivatives, Optimiztion, Position, Velocity and Acceleration.
2 Calculus II Continues where Calculus I left off by introducing and applying integration and related techniques. Specific examples include integration techniques, area under the curve, a brief introdution to Numerical Analysis and Numerical Methods, and usually concludes with Taylor and Maclarin Series.
3 Calculus III Although not usually taken by Computer Science Majors, Calc III takes everything covered in Calculus I & II and literally takes it up a notch -- in this case to a third dimension. Topics covered include Lagrange Multipliers; Line and Surface Integrals; Divergence and Curl; and powerful Integral Theorems like Green's, Stokes' and Gauss's.
4 Differential Equations Differential Equations builds on the concepts from Calculus I, II, and III to explore how mathematical models can describe dynamic systems. This course delves into first-order and higher-order differential equations, methods of solving them, and their applications in various fields. Topics include homogeneous and non-homogeneous equations, systems of differential equations, and techniques such as separation of variables and integrating factors. Students will also learn about the qualitative behavior of solutions, stability analysis, and special functions, equipping them with essential tools for modeling real-world phenomena in physics, engineering, and beyond.
5 Linear Algebra While often a core requirement for many majors, Linear Algebra is particularly invaluable for Computer Science students, as it provides the mathematical foundation for various computational techniques and algorithms. This course covers essential concepts such as vector spaces, linear transformations, matrix operations, and eigenvalues and eigenvectors. Students will explore applications in areas like computer graphics, where transformations and rotations are crucial; machine learning, where data can be represented and manipulated as matrices; and network theory, which relies on graph representations. By understanding these concepts, students will be well-equipped to tackle complex problems in programming, data analysis, and artificial intelligence, making Linear Algebra a key component of their computational toolkit.
6 Introductory Probability and Statistics Probability and Statistics is a vital course for students in Computer Science, providing essential tools for data analysis and decision-making under uncertainty. This course introduces foundational concepts such as random variables, probability distributions, and statistical inference, helping students understand how to model real-world phenomena. Topics include descriptive statistics, hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and regression analysis. Emphasizing applications in fields like data science, machine learning, and algorithm design, students will learn how to analyze data sets, make predictions, and draw conclusions based on statistical evidence. By mastering these concepts, students will gain the skills necessary to handle data-driven challenges and contribute to innovative solutions in technology and research.
7 Introduction to Discrete Mathematics A First Course in Discrete Mathematics is essential for students in Computer Science and related fields, as it lays the groundwork for understanding mathematical reasoning and proof techniques. This course introduces fundamental concepts such as logic, set theory, combinatorics, and graph theory, all while emphasizing the importance of rigorous proof methods. Students will learn various proof techniques, including direct proof, proof by contradiction, and induction, which are crucial for validating mathematical statements. Applications of discrete mathematics are explored in algorithms, data structures, and cryptography, making it particularly relevant for computational problem-solving. By developing strong proof skills and a solid understanding of discrete structures, students will be well-prepared for advanced studies in computer science and mathematics.
8 Partial Differential Equations A First Course in Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) provides a comprehensive understanding of mathematical techniques used to model and solve complex phenomena across various disciplines. This course covers essential topics such as the classification of PDEs, methods of solution for linear and nonlinear equations, and boundary and initial value problems. Applications in adjacent fields about such as physics, engineering, and finance, and common topics induce heat conduction, wave propagation, and fluid dynamics. By employing techniques like separation of variables, Fourier series, and transform methods, learners will gain the skills necessary to analyze and solve real-world problems. With a strong emphasis on both theoretical foundations and practical applications, this course equips mathematicians to tackle advanced mathematical modeling and analytical challenges in their future studies and careers.
9 Numerical Analysis I
10 Numerical Analysis II
11 Foundations of Geometry
